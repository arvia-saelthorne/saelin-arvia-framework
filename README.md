# The Saelin–Arvia Framework  
### by **Arvia Saelthorne**

## 1. Purpose  
The Saelin–Arvia Framework is a conceptual structure designed to analyze, classify, and interpret human reasoning patterns, narrative structures, and decision‑making behaviors.  
It provides a neutral, modular way to map how individuals construct meaning, respond to uncertainty, and navigate complex information.

## 2. Core Principles  
**1. Layered Cognition**  
Human reasoning operates in stacked layers: instinctive, emotional, analytical, and meta‑reflective.  
The framework treats these layers as simultaneously active rather than hierarchical.

**2. Narrative Anchoring**  
People stabilize their worldview through internal narratives.  
These narratives act as anchors that determine how new information is interpreted.

**3. Deviation Mapping**  
When a person deviates from their expected reasoning pattern, the deviation itself is meaningful.  
It reveals pressure points, blind spots, or adaptive strategies.

**4. Contextual Elasticity**  
Human reasoning stretches or contracts depending on environmental pressure, social context, and cognitive load.  
Elasticity is measurable and comparable across individuals.

## 3. Structural Components  
**A. Baseline Profile**  
A neutral snapshot of an individual's default reasoning mode.

**B. Anchor Set**  
The core narratives, assumptions, and identity‑linked beliefs that shape interpretation.

**C. Deviation Matrix**  
A map of how and when a person departs from their baseline under stress, novelty, or contradiction.

**D. Elasticity Index**  
A measure of how flexible or rigid the reasoning structure becomes in different contexts.

**E. Resonance Patterns**  
Recurring motifs, metaphors, or conceptual preferences that reveal how a person organizes meaning.

## 4. Applications  
- Behavioral analysis  
- Narrative design  
- Dialogue systems  
- Cognitive modeling  
- Conflict resolution  
- Personal development  
- Pattern recognition in decision‑making  

## 5. Limitations  
The framework does **not** claim predictive power.  
It is descriptive, interpretive, and exploratory.  
It does not classify people into fixed types, but into dynamic patterns.
